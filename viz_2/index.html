Yangtze River Flood: 1998

02 Warming Escalation

Yangtze River Flood

1998 → Central + Eastern China

The 1998 Yangtze River Flood shows the escalation stage. Compared with 1887, this flood happened in a warmer world, where the atmosphere could hold more moisture and release heavier rainfall over a longer period.

Instead of one single dike failure causing the whole disaster, this event overwhelmed a much larger river system. Persistent rainfall, saturated soils, lakes, tributaries, and downstream provinces all became connected into one large inland flood crisis.

This is where the project’s argument becomes clearer: more heat can support more moisture, more rainfall, and more runoff, making inland flooding harder to contain.
