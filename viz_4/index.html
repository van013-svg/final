Pakistan Super Floods

2010 → Indus River Basin

The 2010 Pakistan floods are the strongest modern inland-flood example in this sequence. Extreme monsoon rainfall overwhelmed the Indus River system and pushed floodwaters across a huge connected basin.

Compared with the earlier floods, Pakistan shows the clearest connection between heat, atmospheric moisture, precipitation, and runoff. The flood did not stay limited to one city or one valley; it moved through mountain regions, agricultural plains, and downstream provinces.

This is the project’s final escalation point: as the atmosphere holds more moisture, rainfall extremes can become more severe, and runoff can turn regional storms into national-scale inland flood disasters.
