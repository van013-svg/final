FROM RAIN TO RUINS

Rainfall nourishes ecosystems and replenishes water supplies. But when precipitation exceeds absorption capacity, flooding transforms entire communities in hours.

01 / THE ACCUMULATION

As global temperatures rise, the atmosphere holds roughly 7% more moisture for every 1°C of warming. When a storm system stalls, it traps massive volumes of precipitable water inside dense, super-saturated cloud columns.

02 / THE TRIGGER

When atmospheric currents shift, the retention threshold breaks. Extreme precipitation dumps weeks worth of water in a matter of hours—far outstripping the ground's natural ability to filter or absorb the sudden weight.

03 / THE IMPACT

Saturated soils trigger immediate, violent runoff. Natural riverbanks breach, flash floods tear through infrastructure, and communities are submerged under a relentless wall of water, transforming vibrant landscapes into ruins.
